Andes Water And REsilience (AWARE)

About the project

"Empowering communities to manage water and climate risks in the Andes" is a VLIR-UOS Short Initiative 2025-2027 project that brings together Ecuadorean universities, water authorities, and UNESCO to enhance decision-making and resilience through community-based monitoring, low-cost sensor technologies, and participatory planning. Focused on both rural and urban case studies, the project delivers tools, training, and co-created solutions to tackle climate variability and support sustainable water management in vulnerable Andean regions.

Objectives

1. Community-based monitoring: Low-cost sensors will be deployed in two case studies—a rural irrigation system in Azuay province and an urban area in Cuenca—to empower stakeholders in monitoring water and climate. Data collected will support a web-based visualization tool.

2. Climate scenarios and stress tests: Scenarios simulating extreme and future conditions (e.g., precipitation variability and temperature rise) will guide stress tests to identify vulnerabilities and operational thresholds in both case studies.

3. Application of UNESCO-CRIDA framework: Co-development of strategies for managing climate risks in a participatory approach, ensuring solutions are locally relevant and technically sound.

4. Capacity building: A regional workshop aimed at technicians and decision-makers from Ecuador and other Andean countries to adopt and replicate methodologies. Graduate students and non-academic technicians will receive targeted training, developing practical skills.
